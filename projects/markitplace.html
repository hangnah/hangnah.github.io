Overview

MarkItPlace is a project that was completed at LA Hacks 2017. The website serves to streamline the process of selling and buying items from people within close proximity of one another. I was responsible for designing the website and creating the style guide for developers to follow.

Design

I started sketching low-fidelity wireframes on Adobe Illustrator, focusing on the home page layout in order to clearly convey MarkItPlace's purpose.

The focal point of the home page is on the map, which pins the locations of items being sold. Users are able to sort through the items via several categories, including the genre of item and price range. The category box is toggled by the hamburger icon in the upper right corner. The mid-fidelity wireframes below explain this feature.

After designing the basic layout of the home page, I created the branding for MarkItPlace, which included the style guide, logo, and language.

Deliverables

The wireframes were presented to the team's developers, who built the website.
