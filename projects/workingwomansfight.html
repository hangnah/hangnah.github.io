A Working Woman’s Fight is a digital humanities project that sheds light on the fight for better working conditions for female LA garment workers, hotel workers, and janitors. My team of five other undergraduate students were tasked with telling their stories using a plethora of data on LA workers ranging from digital media to intake forms.

I was responsible for building and designing the final website. This project was part of my capstone class for the digital humanities program at UCLA. We completed the project within 10 weeks and presented our project to the program directors at the end of the quarter.

Design

The designs were driven by our initial astonishment at the plight of these working women.

When my team received all the documents and intake forms, we were initially overwhelmed and unsure of where to start. The media coverages of protests and other labor movements were powerful, and we wanted to properly tell their stories and give credit to their perseverance. As the rest of my team compiled video footage and created data visualizations to help tell these stories, I worked with the other designer on my team to design an online website to house their work.

My teammate and I wanted the home page of the website to reflect the magnitude and power of these labor movements. We decided to use a combination of animations, static images, and sound to engage visiting users and convey the empowerment of these women in the many campaigns they led and took part in.

Animations were used to maintain movement and were juxtaposed with static images to reflect the struggle between the labor unions and the forces that tried to stop them. Hovering over some of the static images revealed more animations, which played in with our theme of making the invisible work force visible. Additionally, I added overlapping background sounds of protests to convey the overwhelming nature of these movements.

Deliverables

After my team put together the other elements for our project, including an infographic on the LA garment workers and map visualizations of protests and manufacturers, I built the website using Bootstrap 3. My team presented the final project to the Digital Humanities department at our end-of-the-year showcase.
