Research & Conceptualization

How can online learning be more interactive and engaging for the user?

Fulcrum Labs is revolutionizing the way learners achieve mastery in various industries through its machine learning platform. Learners are able to learn at their own pace, and are presented material based on their performance throughout the course. Learners are presented with modules of either text or video, and are given assessments at the end of each section.

Part of my research for this project stems from a psychology course on perception and learning that I took at UCLA. Perceptual learning is fostered through sensations and forms the foundation of cognitive processes, and while there are many theories for learning, one effective method for processing material is through note-taking because it visually organizes information and reduces the learner’s cognitive load during the lesson. Additionally, environments influence retention of material, as some environments can distract learners from fully processing information.

The simple interface of the adaptive learning platform is conducive to learning, but I wanted to explore the option of adding interactive elements to the product to increase learner retention. Currently, the only interactions users have with the platform are to read, watch, and practice the material through a quiz. My objective was to introduce a note-taking system and a dashboard customization feature to make learning more engaging for the user.

Design I

  • Note Taking
  • Dashboard Customization

The native note-taking system would allow users to directly interact with the text module. After highlighting a section of text with the user’s cursor, the “Highlight” or “Add Notes” icon would prompt the user to either only highlight the text or highlight and add a note to the text. A yellow indicator would appear next to the section after the user highlighted the text or added a note. Users would be able to navigate to a separate “Notes” page to view all their notes.

This feature would give users the option to customize their dashboard with themes or colors. The popup would be accessible through a link on the lower left corner of the screen.

User Testing

Adding notes wasn’t as intuitive as I thought, and users had trouble customizing their themes.

  • Note Taking
  • Dashboard Customization

Both users were confused on how to interact with the text and take notes. Although both the highlighter icon lit up and the plus button appeared next to the text after the user highlighted a section of text, the users navigated only to the highlight button. Considering this interaction, I decided to make the “Add Notes” button larger and present it on the right side of the screen at all times.

I asked the users to select a theme, and both of the users did not know about the second page of themes until prompted to check. Additionally, the users weren’t able to immediately locate the link to customize their dashboards. After the test, I gamified the dashboard customization by making the customization modal pop up after the user went further into the lesson.

Reflection

Communicate well. Test early and often.

Throughout the design process, I recognized the importance of user testing to either support or undermine my design decisions. In the research and discussion phase of these two ideas, I was relying on assumptions and feedback from the rest of the product team to drive my design decisions. With user testing, I was able to determine whether the features I wanted to add were being used in the way I designed them to be. Ultimately, after meeting with the product design team and discussing the viability of my design propositions, we decided to scrap the idea of customization, as it didn’t seem to add as much value to the product as the note-taking feature.

Being able to take ownership of this project from research to testing was exciting, and I am grateful to the product team at Fulcrum Labs for giving me the opportunity to do so. While I learned a great deal about collaborating between the design team and receiving feedback on designs, one thing I would have done differently is communicate my ideas earlier and more often throughout the project. That would have helped me hone in one product feature rather than two.

At the end of my internship, I delivered my research, wireframes, and prototypes to the team.
